Choripan

Description

Spicy chorizo drizzled with fresh chimichurri sauce on a nice toasted baguette

Ingredients:

  • Chimichurri:

    • 2 cups packed fresh flat-leaf parsley leaves and tender stems
    • 2 cups packed fresh cilantro leaves and tender stems
    • ⅔ cup extra-virgin olive oil
    • ¼ cup red wine vinegar
    • 2 tablespoons chopped shallot (from 1 small shallot)
    • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano leaves
    • 1 teaspoon chopped garlic (about 1 clove)
    • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
    • ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper
    • ½ teaspoon lime zest (from 1 lime)

  • Sandwhiches

    • 1 lb of argentinian style chorizo
    • 2 Crispy french baguettes

Directions

  • Chimichurri

    1. Finely chop the parsely, oregano, parsely, and cilantro
    2. Mince the garlic and onion
    3. in a large bowl, mix the herbs, vegetables, olive oil, salt, pepper, and lime until it is consistent

  • Sandwhiches

    1. Preheat a grill to about 450 degrees Fahrenheit
    2. Once grill is hot enough, place the chorizos diagnonally on the grill for nice grill marks, and close the lid
    3. check the chorizos every few minutes and rotate them. Pressing on the chorizo slightly to determine softness will indicate how done they are. Depending on the size of the chorizo, this will take between 8 to 10 minutes
    4. while chorizos are cooking, slice your baguette into portions roughly in size of the chorizo
    5. when chorizos are almost done, place on the grill to char and soften up the baguette
    6. when the chorizos are done, slice them in half and place on the toasted baguette
    7. drizzle the chimichurri on top of the chorizo to taste
